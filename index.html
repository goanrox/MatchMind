soccer confusion

Hover each card for the real answer — explained using sports you already love.

"Why is everyone celebrating a 0-0 tie??"
↑ Hover for the answer
The Answer
A 0-0 game is often a tactical war — two defenses refusing to crack. Keeping a clean sheet can be a massive result.
🏈 American Analogy

Like a 3-0 NFL game where the defense dominated every drive — ugly score, huge pride.

😵
"What even is offside? I've asked 10 times."
↑ Hover for the answer
The Answer
You're offside if you're too far ahead of the defense at the exact moment the pass is played to you.
🏒 American Analogy

Like getting ahead of the play before the puck arrives. Timing is everything.

🤷
"Why did the clock go past 90 minutes??"
↑ Hover for the answer
The Answer
The ref adds stoppage time to make up for injuries, substitutions, reviews, and delays.
🏀 American Analogy

Think of it like adding back dead time at the end of a quarter, but less exact.

🥱
"Why did the game just stop for a VIDEO REPLAY??"
↑ Hover for the answer
The Answer
That’s VAR — officials review key moments like goals, penalties, and red cards using video replay.
🏈 American Analogy

It’s basically instant replay, but fans get just as annoyed waiting for the decision.

😳
"Why did they get a 1-on-1 shot after a foul??"
↑ Hover for the answer
The Answer
That’s a penalty kick — one player against the goalkeeper after a foul inside the box.
🏀 American Analogy

Like a pressure-packed free throw, except way more dramatic and with a goalkeeper staring you down.

😂
"Why is he rolling around like he's dying??"
↑ Hover for the answer
The Answer
Sometimes players exaggerate contact to win a foul. It’s controversial — and refs do punish obvious simulation.
🏀 American Analogy

Think NBA flopping, just with more drama and more rolling.

like American sports

The simple version, without the jargon overload.

⏱️

How Time Works

Two 45-minute halves with stoppage time added at the end. The clock doesn’t stop the way it does in American sports.

Beginner essential
🟨

Yellow & Red Cards

Yellow is a warning. Red means immediate ejection, and the team plays a man down.

Know this fast
🏆

Extra Time & Shootouts

When knockout games are tied after 90, they go to extra time, then penalties if needed.
